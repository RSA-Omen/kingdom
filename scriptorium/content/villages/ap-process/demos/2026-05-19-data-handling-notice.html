Subject
Data Handling Assessment — Claude / Anthropic for AP Processing

Purpose

This notice sets out the data-handling implications of using Anthropic's Claude AI to extract information from supplier invoices and supporting documents within the AP Processing workflow. It is intended to give management a clear, documented basis for approving (or constraining) the use of this service before it is rolled into production.

Summary Position

Use of the Anthropic API for invoice data extraction is assessed as acceptable for our intended scope, provided the controls listed in Section 6 are implemented. The risk profile is comparable to other cloud-based document-processing tools we already use, and is materially lower than uploading documents to consumer AI products (e.g. ChatGPT free tier, public chatbots).

What Data Is Sent to Anthropic

When an invoice is processed, the following information is transmitted to Anthropic's API:

  • Supplier name, ABN / VAT number, contact details
  • Banking details shown on the invoice (BSB, account number, SWIFT)
  • Invoice number, date, line items, descriptions, prices, GST
  • Our company's billing address and any PO references
  • Free-text remarks present on the document

No internal Pronto data, customer data, employee records, or system credentials are sent.

Anthropic's Data-Handling Commitments

The following commitments are contractually binding under Anthropic's Commercial Terms of Service:

ItemPosition
Training on our data No. API inputs and outputs are not used to train Anthropic's models.
Retention Up to 30 days for abuse monitoring, then deleted. Zero Data Retention (ZDR) available on request for enterprise customers.
Human review None, unless content is flagged by automated abuse detection systems.
Infrastructure Hosted on AWS (Anthropic's sole sub-processor). Default region is the United States.
Compliance SOC 2 Type II certified. GDPR Data Processing Addendum available.

Identified Risks

  1. Prompt injection via malicious documents. A supplier (or attacker impersonating one) could embed hidden instructions in a PDF designed to manipulate Claude's output — e.g. inflating an amount or substituting bank details. This is the most material risk.
  2. Data residency. Default processing occurs in the United States. For suppliers based in jurisdictions with data-export restrictions, this may require disclosure.
  3. Accidental over-sharing. Sending entire documents when only specific fields are required increases unnecessary exposure.
  4. Provider availability and continuity. Anthropic is a third-party service; outages or terms changes are outside our control.

Required Controls

The following controls are recommended as conditions of use:

  • Human-in-the-loop confirmation. No extracted invoice data is to be entered into Pronto Xi without operator review and approval. This control directly mitigates the prompt-injection risk.
  • Direct API use only. The Anthropic API is to be accessed directly by our application — not through third-party wrappers or aggregators that introduce additional data handlers.
  • Zero Data Retention (ZDR) request. ZDR will be requested from Anthropic, removing the 30-day retention window entirely once granted.
  • Local storage of source documents. Original PDFs and emails remain in our own storage (~/Operations/ap-processing/storage/). Anthropic receives only the document content needed for extraction and retains no persistent copy.
  • Audit trail. Every call to the Anthropic API is logged (timestamp, document reference, fields requested) for compliance and troubleshooting purposes.
  • Scoped data. Where feasible, documents will be pre-processed to send only the pages or regions needed for extraction.

What This Is Not

For clarity:

  • Claude is not authorised to enter data into Pronto autonomously.
  • Claude is not authorised to approve or release payments.
  • Claude does not retain knowledge of prior invoices between requests; each call is independent.
  • This assessment covers invoice extraction only. Any expansion of Claude's role in AP (e.g. supplier communication, payment release) requires a fresh review.

Recommendation

Management approval is sought to proceed with Anthropic's Claude as the extraction engine for AP invoice processing, subject to the controls in Section 6 being implemented before production rollout.
