AP Processing — where we are

An autonomous pipeline from email to Pronto AP Entry, with Finance keeping the final approval.

Status as of 2026-05-17 · Owner: Lauchlan

What it does

The finance team forwards (or saves) AP-related emails into the app. The system unwraps every attachment, splits multi-page scans into individual documents, classifies each one (tax invoice, delivery docket, statement, PO, etc.), extracts every relevant field with Claude, resolves the supplier to a Pronto code against the live supplier master, and queues everything for Finance to review.

Finance does the last step — confirm and post. The system never writes to Pronto without a human pressing the button.

  • Multi-document scans split into individual invoices/dockets (the splitter uses Claude vision)

For the AP team — what we'd love your input on

The pipeline above does the mechanical work (unwrap, classify, extract, supplier-match). It does not — and should not — guess your AP policy. A few decisions will shape the next phase. Please react to anything that's wrong; we'd rather change it now than after we build.

Our assumed AP lifecycle

Every invoice flows through these stages once it lands in the app. The app's job is to close as many of these as possible before showing the invoice to a human, then surface the ones that need attention.

  1. Receipt — arrives by email or scanner. done
  2. Identification — supplier matched to the Pronto code. done · 87% auto on first batch
  3. Coding — invoice gets linked to the right thing in the GL: a Purchase Order, an expense account, a project code, etc. next
  4. Validation — for PO-backed invoices, three-way match: invoice header matches PO header, invoice lines match goods received. next
  5. Approval — under threshold = auto-clear; over threshold = sent to a named approver. later
  6. Posting — written into Pronto AP Entry, paid in the next run. later (Bender drives)

Decisions we need from AP

QuestionWhy it matters
What % of invoices have a PO vs direct-pay?Drives whether PO matching is the main path or a side-path. Direct-pay (utilities, subscriptions) needs GL coding logic instead.
Approval thresholds — who approves what amount?The app routes the right invoices to the right person. Wrong rules = bottlenecks.
For non-PO invoices, what's the coding source?Spreadsheet? Memory? Vendor-default? Determines if we can pre-fill GL codes too.
What invoices get parked most often, and why?"Disputed", "missing PO", "wrong supplier name" — knowing the top three failure modes lets us design for them, not against them.
Is anyone approving on mobile / outside hours?If yes, the approval step needs an email / Teams notification, not just an inbox.
How does Pronto check tax / GST on invoices?If Pronto auto-calculates tax from the supplier code, we don't need to extract it. If AP enters tax per line, we do.

What the AP team will not have to do

  • Re-type any field from a PDF — extraction is already accurate enough that the data is pre-filled
  • Hunt for the supplier code — it's resolved before they see the invoice
  • Open multiple browser tabs to compare invoice / docket / PO — the side panel shows all three together
  • Worry about losing the original PDF — every email and attachment is archived outside the working code

Design principle that anchors all of this

Agents observe, analyse, and recommend. The human keeps the final action. "Autonomous" means everything up to the Approve button runs without a human in the loop — pressing the button stays with Finance, always. This is a hard guarantee, not a configurable preference.
