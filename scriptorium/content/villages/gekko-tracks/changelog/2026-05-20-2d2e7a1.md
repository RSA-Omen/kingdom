# docs(incidents): May 2026 batches hidden across all UIs — post-mortem

## What changed

A single new file: `docs/incidents/2026-05-20-may-batches-hidden.md` — the full incident report for the May 2026 batches that went invisible across cardholder and finance views.

## Why

A cardholder reported missing inbox transactions; logging in as Finance reproduced the gap. The 311 May transactions and 25 cardholder batches were present in the database the whole time — three compounding defects across the importer, the release flow, and the inbox filter silently hid them from every UI surface.

## The three defects

1. **Importer mis-stamped `import_job_id`.** Rows from `Data_export_18052026.csv` (job 5) were stamped with `import_job_id=1` (the old UAT seed) instead of 5. Job 5 ended up with zero transactions pointing back to it.
2. **The "release to cardholders" flow attached batches to the wrong finance parent.** Because the May rows looked (per their wrong `import_job_id`) like they belonged to job 1, the 25 new cardholder batches were parented to finance batch `id=29` titled `"TestData"`.
3. **The cardholder inbox silently filtered any batch whose parent title contained `"test"`.** `backend/app/routers/cardholders.py:444` does a naive `"test" in title.lower()` substring match. `"TestData".lower()` matches, so all 25 cardholder batches were dropped from every inbox response without explanation.

## Resolution

Live database repair, applied atomically. No code change, no service restart.

| # | Action | Rows |
|---|--------|------|
| 1 | Renamed finance batch `id=29` from `"TestData"` → `"UAT seed data"` (quick unblock) | 1 |
| 2 | Created finance batch `id=128`: `title="2026_05"`, `import_job_id=5`, `status="released"` | 1 |
| 3 | Re-parented cardholder batches `103..127` from `parent_batch_id=29` → `parent_batch_id=128` | 25 |
| 4 | Backfilled `transactions.import_job_id` from `1` → `5` for the 311 transactions in scope | 311 |

## Verification (post-repair)

- `GET /api/cardholders/28/inbox` — Wayne sees batch 123 with parent `"2026_05"` under "Completed".
- `GET /api/cardholders/22/inbox` — Simon sees batch 119 with parent `"2026_05"`, `status="open"`, 6 transactions.
- `GET /api/finance/inbox` — import job 5 reports `transaction_count=311`, `total_debit=$61,533.87`, `status="released"`, `finance_batch_id=128`.
- `GET /api/finance/batch-manager` — `"2026_05"` parent appears with 25 children and 311 transactions.

## Outstanding follow-ups

- **High** — Replace the `"test" in title` substring filter at `backend/app/routers/cardholders.py:434,444` with a status-based check (`parent_batch.status == "archived"`). UAT/test data should be hidden by being archived, not by title heuristic.
- **High** — Forensic on the importer code path that mis-stamps `import_job_id`. Reproduce against a fresh DB to confirm the hypothesis.
- **Medium** — Integration test asserting a newly imported CSV stamps `transactions.import_job_id` correctly for every row.
- **Medium** — Structured logging when the cardholder inbox skips a batch (reason + batch id + parent id).
- **Low** — Sanity check in the "release to cardholders" flow that the parent finance batch's `import_job_id` matches the transactions' actual job.

## Lessons

1. **Substring matches on operator-supplied strings are fragile.** Any title containing the word "test" — `"TestData"`, `"Latest"`, `"Contest"` — gets caught. Use explicit status fields for behaviour.
2. **Silent filters hide bugs.** When the inbox dropped 25 batches, no log line said why.
3. **Data on the wire being correct ≠ data on the screen being correct.** The DB had the right rows the whole time. Three independent layers conspired to hide them.
