Receipts — capture, OCR, match

A receipt enters the system from a phone camera or a file upload, gets OCR'd, and is scored against unmatched transactions on the cardholder's card. High-score matches attach automatically; low-score matches surface for the cardholder to pick.

Notes

  • Camera capture is the primary path. The phone form-factor is the right place to deal with paper receipts because that's where they physically are.
  • Hash + dedup is the first step. Re-uploading the same receipt does not create a duplicate or re-run OCR.
  • The matcher refuses to guess. Low-confidence matches go to manual_review rather than auto-attaching to the wrong transaction.
  • Reassignment is supported as a first-class workflow. Shared-card cases happen and the system makes them tractable.
  • Line-level matching is opt-in evidence. Not required for a match, but used to strengthen borderline header-level scores.
